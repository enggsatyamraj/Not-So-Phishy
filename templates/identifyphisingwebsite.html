Preventing phishing is crucial to safeguard your personal information and sensitive data. Here are general ways to help prevent falling victim to phishing attempts

Safeguarding one's personal information and protecting sensitive data in the digital age is of paramount importance.

Education and Awareness

Educate yourself and others about phishing techniques, common phishing signs, and how to recognize suspicious emails, messages, and websites.

Verify Sender Information:

Always verify the sender's email address and domain to ensure it matches the legitimate organization's domain.

Think Before You Click

Be cautious with links in emails, especially if the email is unexpected, the URL looks suspicious, or the email conveys a sense of urgency or pressure to click.

Check for Generic Greetings:

Be suspicious of emails that use generic greetings (e.g., "Dear Customer") instead of your name or specific details.

Use Multi-Factor Authentication:

Enable MFA wherever possible to add an extra layer of security when accessing accounts or platforms.

Avoid Sharing Personal Info:

Refrain from sharing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or social security numbers via email or on unsecured websites.
